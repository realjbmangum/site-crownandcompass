Hero Video Prompts

Brotherhood, moving.

Five detailed directions for the home-page hero as a slow, living loop. The whole point is encouragement: rugged, everyday men building each other up. Groups of guys, not pairs. Backslaps, shoulder grips, high-fives, an arm thrown around a shoulder at the top of a climb. It should feel like iron sharpening iron, grounded and masculine, never staged, never intimate. Paste a prompt below into your AI video tool, and paste the negative prompt into the tool's negative field if it has one, that is what keeps it from drifting soft or slick. Generate a couple, keep the one that hits, save it in public as hero.mp4, and I will wire it behind the headline.

The shared look (already written into every prompt)

Cinematic documentary footage on 35mm film with warm halation and fine grain, in the style of a Terrence Malick or a rugged outdoor brand film. The men are real, weathered, everyday guys, not models: beards, work-worn hands, flannel shirts, canvas and waxed-cotton jackets, denim and boots. The feeling is brotherhood and encouragement, strong and grounded masculine camaraderie, men who have each other's backs. Candid and unposed, caught mid-moment, never staged, never romantic or tender, never a music-video slow-motion cliche. Naturalistic low-key lighting, deep shadows, a muted earthy palette of ink black, bone cream, warm amber and ember orange. Anatomically correct people with natural undistorted hands and faces. Slow, steady, deliberate camera movement built to loop seamlessly, with the left third of the frame kept darker and open for a headline overlay.

  • Composition first. The men and the warm light belong on the right; keep the left third dark and open, that is where the headline lands.
  • Keep guys in groups of three to five. Two men alone in firelight is where these tools drift soft. A group reads as a brotherhood.
  • Frame the gesture at medium distance. Close-up high-fives and handshakes are where AI mangles hands. A medium-wide shot keeps the encouragement clear and the hands clean.
  • Ask for a seamless loop and a single continuous shot, no cuts. If your tool cannot loop, a slow push-in loops acceptably.
