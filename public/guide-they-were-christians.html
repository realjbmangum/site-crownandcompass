Reading Guide

They Were Christians

Cristobal Krusen

A simple guideBeing
Get the book

What it is about

Krusen gathers short biographies of notable men and women from history and shows how their Christian faith shaped the choices they made and the mark they left, in politics, science, the arts, and service.

The stories are a reminder that ordinary believers can live with courage and conviction in the middle of real challenges.

The big idea

Christian faith reaches past private belief. Lived out with honesty and perseverance, it shapes history and touches lives for good.

Talk it through

  1. Which story in the book stayed with you most, and why did it hit close to home?
  2. When has your own faith been tested in the work you do or the responsibilities you carry?
  3. What obstacles in your daily life make it hard to live out what you believe?
  4. How does hearing about these figures change the way you think about your own influence as a husband, father, or leader?
  5. Where in your family or community do you sense a quiet call to step up with courage?
  6. What small habit of faith have you seen make a difference over time in your life or someone close to you?
  7. Who might need to hear one of these stories from you this week, and what holds you back from sharing it?

Read the Word

Matthew 5:13-16 James 2:14-17 Ephesians 2:8-10 1 Peter 2:11-12 Galatians 6:9-10

One practice

Pick one person from the book, or from your own life, whose faith you admire. Spend a few quiet minutes each morning remembering one specific way they lived it out. Then look for one small opportunity in your day to do something similar in your own ordinary setting. Keep it simple and steady.
