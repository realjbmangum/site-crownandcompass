The 100-Day Planner.

Fourteen weeks. Four phases. One day at a time. This is the shape of the walk I followed when the fog was thick and my hands were not steady. Print it, put it somewhere you will see it each morning, and write it down as you go.

Back to resources

One hundred days is long enough for your brain to lay down a new path, and short enough to hold in your hands. The walk moves through four phases. In the first weeks the body clears and the days feel long. Then comes the furnace, the hardest stretch, where the old pattern fights to come back. After that the fog lifts and you start building again. In the last two weeks you cross the line and look at who you have become.

Each week carries a theme and one practice to keep. Under it, a row for every day. You owe it three small things: the day, one honest line on how it went, and a check that you kept the practice. That is the whole system. Simple enough to follow on a foggy morning, strong enough to hold you when the day turns sharp.

Phase One: The Fog

Weeks 9 to 12 · Days 57 to 84

Phase Three: The Clearing

The fog lifts and something creative stirs. The question turns from what you are quitting to what you are building. You start again.

Weeks 13 to 14 · Days 85 to 100

The rhythm is yours now. Cross the line, look at the man you have become, and decide what the next hundred days will hold.

This planner walks with the four pillars from the book, Body, Being, Balance, and Business, and follows the same arc: sedation, the whisper, the furnace, the compass, the return. None of it replaces a brother. Print a copy, and bring it to your chapter.
